The book

A novel that presents itself, formally, as a situation report — filed by a narrator who has spent over a decade in perpetual intercontinental transit and never stayed anywhere a full month. Interior, digressive, observational; geography is memory, and memory will not hold still.

“I travelled between countries for work, never staying longer than a month in the same place. That had been my life for over ten years now.”

Its frame warns the reader plainly: anyone hoping for drama, tragedy and an epic arc will be disappointed — supplying one would falsify the reality of F. Wendelin. The flatness is not a deficit. It is the diagnosis.

The author

Gabriel De La Cruz writes from inside the borderless managerial world the book anatomises — a German writer of Greek-Filipino parentage. The result is less a novel of place than a report on the impossibility of place.
